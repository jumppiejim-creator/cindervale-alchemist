Chapter 1: Game Overview

Cindervale Alchemist is a single-player browser RPG where you run an alchemy shop across four unique locations. Brew potions, inscribe enchantments, forage ingredients, manage staff, build your workshop, and invest in your settlement. When your character reaches their peak, Pass the Torch and start a new generation carrying forward your legacy.

Core Features

  • 4 Locations — Cindervale (volcanic), Ashfall Crossing (desert), Tidecrest Harbor (coastal), and Skyreach Peaks (alpine). Each has 8 foraging regions, 4 factions, 3 external threats, and unique ingredients.
  • 5 Base Classes with 15 Specializations — Alchemist, Enchanter, Artificer, Scholar, and Warden. Each class has 3 specialization paths unlocked at level 3.
  • 9 Prestige Classes — Advanced multiclass paths with mixed prerequisites combining class levels, skill ranks, and feats.
  • 15 Factions — 3-4 per location (plus 3 external threats per location). Build reputation through quests, donations, and alignment for unique bonuses.
  • 62 Feats in 8 categories — Permanent abilities earned one per level.
  • D20 Skill System — 17 skills across 5 ability scores. Roll d20 + modifier vs. DC for crafting, foraging, enchanting, social checks, and more.
  • Pass the Torch — Retire your character and begin a new generation, carrying forward gold, recipes, reputation, and a legacy chain.

The Game Loop

Each day you have a pool of Energy (base 4, increased by upgrades, class features, and feats) and Hours (base 4). Spend them on actions:

  • Brew Potions — Combine ingredients, roll a craft check vs. DC. Success produces potions to sell, shelf, or use in quests.
  • Forage Ingredients — Travel to a region, spend hours extracting. Roll extraction checks per hour for ingredients.
  • Enchant Items — Customers bring items for enchantment. Roll inscription checks to inscribe runes for gold.
  • Research — Study to discover new recipes and enchantment patterns.
  • Manage Staff — Hire apprentices and assign them to brew, forage, shopkeep, or research.
  • Complete Quests — NPCs post quests requiring specific potions, ingredients, or tasks.
  • Invest in Settlement — Build roads, markets, watchtowers, and more to improve the town.

Progression

Gain XP from every action. Level up to earn class levels, skill points (3 per level), and feats (1 per level). Choose specializations at class level 3. Pursue prestige classes when you meet their prerequisites. Build toward the Hollow March endgame event, then Pass the Torch to begin anew.

Where Did Merchant Go?

The Merchant class has been removed in the V2 balance patch. Its mechanics have been rehomed across the game for better integration:

  • Caravans & supply chains → Warden Quartermaster specialization
  • Apprentice training bonuses → Master Trainer feat
  • Faction harmony & diplomacy → Diplomat prestige class
  • Brand creation & product lines → Brand Master prestige class
  • Customer bonuses & sell price increases → Economic feats (Regular Clientele, Silver Tongue, Patron of the Arts, etc.)

This consolidation reduces class count from 6 to 5 while preserving every meaningful Merchant mechanic in a more flexible, multiclass-friendly form.

Chapter 11: Core Systems & Travel

Crafting (Brewing)

To brew a potion, you need the required ingredients and enough hours. Roll d20 + stat modifier + relevant craft skill rank + bonuses vs. the recipe's DC.

  • Natural 1 — Auto-fail. Ingredients consumed (unless salvage applies).
  • Natural 20 — Auto-success. Masterwork quality (2x value).
  • Beat DC by 5+ — Fine quality (1.5x value).
  • Beat DC by 10+ — Masterwork quality (2x value).

Each recipe is associated with a primary stat (CRE, INU, ACU, or TEC). Your craft bonus comes from that stat's modifier plus your rank in the matching craft skill (Improvisation, Attunement, Research, or Precision).

Foraging (Extraction)

Travel to a region (costs travel time in hours), then spend remaining hours extracting ingredients. Each hour, roll d20 + Extraction skill vs. the region's DC.

  • Success — Gain ingredients from the region's loot table.
  • Failure — Gain nothing for that hour (or 1 common ingredient at Warden 9+).
  • Natural 20 — Critical extraction: bonus rare ingredients.

Different foraging sites within a region weight ingredients differently, allowing you to target specific materials.

Enchanting (Inscription)

Customers bring items for enchantment. Roll d20 + Inscription skill vs. the enchantment pattern's DC. Requires materials (reduced by Enchanter features and workshop upgrades).

  • Success — Earn gold from the customer. Beat DC by 5+ for Fine quality (1.5x gold). Beat by 10+ for Masterwork (2x gold).
  • Failure — No payment. Materials consumed (unless recycler effects apply).

Research

Spend hours in research studies to discover new recipes and enchantment patterns. Discovery chance is based on your Research skill rank, Innovation skill, and various bonuses. Scholar class features dramatically increase discovery rates.

Experiments

Combine ingredients at the Experiment Bench to try to discover new recipes. More speculative than research but can discover recipes not available through study.

Travel

Traveling to a foraging region costs hours based on the region's distance. Base travel times range from 1h (easy regions) to 4h (legendary regions).

V2 Travel Note

No skill passively reduces travel time. Travel reduction sources:

  • Warden class features — -1h at Lv 1 (Trailblazer), -3h total at Lv 10 (Legend of the Wild: all travel becomes 0).
  • Trailblazer's Boots feat — -1h to all regions.

Settlement projects (Paved Roads) and faction bonuses (e.g., Dustwalkers Tier 1, Pearl Divers Tier 2) can also reduce travel time in specific locations.

Energy & Hours

Each day you start with a pool of Energy (base 4) and Hours (base 4). Most actions cost 1 Energy and some number of hours. When you run out of either, your day is done.

  • Brewing — 1 Energy, 1 hour (some recipes take more).
  • Foraging — 1 Energy, travel time + gathering hours.
  • Enchanting — 1 Energy, 1 hour per customer.
  • Research — 1 Energy, 1-2 hours.
  • Quests — 1 Energy to turn in.

Staff Management

Hire apprentices to delegate tasks. Staff can brew, forage, shopkeep, or research. They have their own stats, traits, and efficiency that improve with XP. Some traits are positive (Quick Learner, Keen Senses) and some negative (Clumsy, Shy). Staff morale affects efficiency.

Shelving & Sales

Place brewed potions on shelves for passive sales. Each morning, customers may purchase shelved potions. Sale chance depends on shelf capacity, potion quality, and various bonuses. Unsold potions remain on shelves until they spoil or sell.

Chapter 12: The Hollow March

The Hollow March is the endgame invasion event that triggers when your character reaches level 10. Reality tears open and waves of crises assault your settlement. Survive 5 waves to unlock Pass the Torch. Survive all 10 for the Final Stand and legendary rewards.

How It Works

  • Waves arrive at intervals (5-7 days for early waves, compressing to 3-4 days for late waves).
  • Each wave presents 2-5 crises that must be resolved before the next wave.
  • Crisis DC starts at 15 (wave 1) and scales to 24 (wave 10).
  • Each crisis has multiple solution paths — you choose based on your strengths.

Crisis Types

CrisisDescription
Veil BreachA tear in reality. Seal it with enchantments, brew repellents, hold the line, or hire mercenaries.
Corruption PlagueMagical sickness spreading. Brew cures, enchant purification wards, forage rare remedies, or fund healers.
Siege AssaultThe horde at the gates. Fortify defenses, enchant armor, lead a sortie, or buy war supplies.
Supply CrisisSupply lines cut. Brew sustenance, emergency forage, deploy staff, or buy provisions.
Morale CollapseFear spreads. Brew courage draughts, rally the people, or stabilize through gold.

Solution Types

Each crisis offers 3-4 solution paths:

  • Skill Check — Roll d20 + relevant skill vs. crisis DC. Beating DC by 5+ earns mastery bonus rewards.
  • Potion Delivery — Provide specific potions (quantity scales with wave).
  • Ingredient Delivery — Provide raw materials for the solution.
  • Gold Payment — Pay mercenaries or healers (cost escalates steeply).
  • Staff Deployment — Send your workforce to handle the crisis.

Consequences

  • Success — XP, gold, and threat reduction per crisis solved.
  • Mastery (beat DC by 5+) — Bonus XP, gold, and extra threat reduction.
  • Failure — Threat spike (+12-22), staff injuries, ingredient loss, gold loss. At wave 6+, workshop upgrades can be disabled for 3 days.

Final Stand (Wave 10)

The ultimate challenge: 6 crises in rapid succession at +4 DC on top of normal wave DC (total DC 28). Completing the Final Stand earns 3000 XP, 800 gold, the title "Defender of the Vale," and permanently caps all threats at 25.

The March and Your Build

Tip: The Crisis Responder feat gives +3 to crisis checks and reduces solution costs. Fortified Workshop slows threat growth and protects your upgrades. A diverse skill set across multiple stats ensures you always have a viable solution path.

At retirement, you choose one feature tree to pass forward. Your options are:

  • Specialization chain — all spec features you unlocked (e.g., Ranger Lv3 + Lv6 + Lv10)
  • Prestige class chain — all prestige features you unlocked (e.g., Cartographer Lv1–5)
  • Re-pass — if you inherited a chain from your own mentor, you can pass it forward again

Every effect in the chosen chain is merged into a single permanent bonus. The new character has these effects active from day 1. They appear in the character sheet under “Mentor’s Legacy” and are aggregated into all relevant checks automatically.

What Does NOT Carry Forward

The new character does not inherit class levels, specializations, or prestige progress. They must earn everything themselves:

  • No inherited class levels — prestige prerequisites must be met through your own progression
  • No inherited specs — specialization panels only appear when you choose your own spec at class level 3
  • No inherited skill ranks — start fresh with 3 skill points at level 1

The legacy feature chain provides passive bonuses (craft bonus, extraction bonus, discovery chance, etc.) but does not shortcut class or prestige progression.

Lineage Record

Each retired character is recorded in your lineage, showing their name, race, classes, specs, and accomplishments. The lineage tracks your dynasty across generations and determines mentor bonuses.

Strategy: Building a Legacy

Choose your legacy chain to complement your next build. A Warden/Ranger chain gives foraging bonuses to a character who plans to focus on Enchanting. A Scholar chain's XP and discovery bonuses help any build level faster. Prestige chains like Cartographer or Siege Engineer provide powerful passive effects without requiring the new character to invest in those prestige paths. Each generation starts with stronger passive bonuses than the last — but the journey is always your own.
